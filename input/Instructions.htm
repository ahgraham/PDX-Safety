Introduction:

            Welcome to the PDX Safety app, this project was designed between the Portland Police Bureau, local business leaders, and high school students from Wilson and Franklin High Schools. The goal of this project is to increase communication between police officers and the community. And more specifically the app was designed to allow officers to communicate directly to the people living in their district. Instructions for the core functionalities of the app (at this time) are listed below. If you have a problem at any point while using the instructions or you have problems with the app, try to submit feedback through the in app “Give Feedback” menu tab. If you can’t do that, send an email to support@pdxsafety.org.

 

Setting Up an Account:

1.     Go to input.pdxsafety.org, if you are not redirected to a login page you are logged in.

2.     You will need to register a new account. Click “Register Here” at the bottom.

3.     Enter in all the information asked for:

a.     The email address you enter should be one you can access while on duty. We will use it to reset your password if you forget it.

b.     We recommend that you use your badge number as your username.

c.      What the Login Password is is your decision, do not share it with anyone.

d.     To create an account you will need a registration password :

                                               i.         The registration password is the same for everyone (during the BETA test, this will most likely change after the BETA test). It is “pdxsafety503” without the quotes.

4.     Once you hit submit you’ll be taken to the login screen. Enter your username and password that you just created to continue.

 

Posting a Message:

1.     Go to input.pdxsafety.org, if you are not redirected to a login page you are logged in.

2.     If you have an account, enter your username and password. If you do not, see the “Setting Up an Account” instructions above.

3.     Once logged in, the first thing to choose is the district number your post is relevant to. Click the precinct whose area your district in is and the click the district number. If your announcement is relevant to the entire city, you can select “City Wide” instead of a district number. BE CAREFUL WHEN SELECTING CITY WIDE. WE DO NOT WANT TO FLOOD THE CITY WIDE ANNOUNCEMENTS WITH IRRELEVANT INFORMATION.

4.     On the next page enter a short title for your message.

5.     Enter a message. The message can be anything you want and it can be as long as you want it to be.

6.     Next at the bottom of this page, select what category your message falls into

a.     “Neighborhood Safety” Go to step 8          

b.     “Announcements” Go to step 7

c.      “BOLO” Go to step 8

7.     If you selected “Announcements” you can categorize it further on the next page. If your announcements does not fall into one of the categories select “Other” and proceed.

a.     If you think your announcement should have a category, submit a feature request through our feedback page, which can be accessed from the menu at the top.

8.     On the final page you will see a preview of all the information you have entered. If you notice any issues you can go back and change that information with the “Previous” button on the left-hand side of the page.

9.     If you are posting a BOLO you MUST enter in the case number associated with that BOLO so it can be tracked in our system and properly removed.

10. If all the information is complete, hit submit.

 

Deleting a BOLO:

1.     Go to input.pdxsafety.org, if you are not redirected to a login page you are logged in.

2.     Select “BOLO’s” in the menu in the top-right of the screen.

3.     You will see a list of all BOLO’s currently active in our system. If you see a BOLO in our system that you responded to and resolved click the red X and follow the instruction in the pop-up window.

 

Additional Help:

            If you are having problems creating an account or logging in ask another officer who has been able to create an account and log in. If that does not solve the problem send an email to support@pdxsafety.org and describe your problem to us.

            For help with what information is allowed to be posted contact Michael Marshman or the Communication Unit.

            If you are experiencing a bug or problem with the program once logged in, submit a feedback report. If your problem is preventing you from using the feedback form, send an email to support@pdxsafety.org.
